Original Questions

Are the risks of underage drinking worth advocating for a change in public opinion and formal legislation for; and if so what would that legislation entail?

I don't believe that the best path forward involves legislation regulating alcohol. Prohibition clearly demonstrated that government regulation of goods tends to only increase the popularity and crime relating to said goods. It seems to me that the legal drinking age should actually be lowered to eighteen years old instead of the current twenty one in order to be more consistent with other legislation designed to protect minors. However, that is not to say that I encourage drinking under the age of twenty one.

How has underage Drinking changed over the years? To what extend has it become more or less prevelant? How has the public opinion on the matter changed?

The academic literature is unclear on this matter: there are many contradicting sources and claims. Anecdotally speaking, it seems to be the case that abstaining from alcohol consumption from the ages of sixteen to twenty is somewhat of an abnormality.

How did we as a society come to the point in which a large portion of our youth partakes in regular recreational drug use?

There is no conclusive evidence to explain this phenomenon. It's clear that alcohol has always been a popular vice, not only in the Western world but around the globe. I conject that its popularity stems from its ease of brewing coupled with its ability to ease angst.

Is anything currently being done to change the status quo: why or why not?

There are plenty of organizations dedicated to the issue of alcohol abuse, for example, Alcoholics Anonymous. Nothing aimed to reduce alcohol consumption in general is currently being done that is worth noting. This is likely due to the popularity of alcohol: it would be extremely difficult for a movement of this sort to gain traction.

Is there a singular root of the issue that can be addressed and combatted?

Alcoholism seems to be a multifaceted issue. Some likely factors are a genetic predisposition to addiction, chronically low serotonin levels, and early exposure to alcohol.
